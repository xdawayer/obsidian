# competitor-alternatives

Source: /Users/wzb/obsidian/marketingskills/skills/competitor-alternatives/SKILL.md

## Skill trigger (prompt intent)
- Use for competitor comparison or alternative pages: “vs page,” “alternative page,” “X vs Y,” “X alternative.”

## Scope in one line
Create SEO-friendly competitor comparison pages that rank and convert by being honest, deep, and structured.

## Prompt structure deep dive
1. Initial Assessment
   - Understand your product, competitors, goals.
2. Core Principles
   - Honest comparisons, depth over surface, decision guidance, modular data.
3. Page Formats (4 types)
   - Competitor alternative (singular)
   - Competitor alternatives (plural)
   - You vs competitor
   - Competitor A vs competitor B
4. Index Pages
   - Hub pages that consolidate comparisons and help SEO discovery.
5. Content Architecture
   - Centralized competitor data files reused across pages.
6. Section Templates
   - TL;DR, paragraphs, feature comparisons, pricing, support, who it’s for, migration, social proof.
7. Research Process
   - Trial products, pricing checks, review mining, customer interviews.
8. SEO Considerations
   - Keyword targeting, internal linking, FAQ schema.
9. Output Format
   - Competitor data file + page content + page set plan.

## Best-fit use cases
- “We need a Notion alternative page.”
- “Create a /vs/ page for top competitors.”
- “Build a comparison hub for SEO.”

## How to use (step-by-step)
1. Define competitor set and goals
   - Choose 3-5 competitors and prioritize search demand.
2. Build centralized competitor data
   - Create a single source of truth per competitor (pricing, features, pros/cons).
3. Select page formats
   - Pick which of the 4 formats to produce and in what order.
4. Draft index pages
   - Build hub pages for discovery and internal linking.
5. Create page content with templates
   - TL;DR, comparisons by category, pricing, support, migration, social proof.
6. Apply SEO considerations
   - Keyword intent, internal links, FAQ schema where relevant.
7. Set maintenance cadence
   - Quarterly pricing checks and annual full refresh.

## Output expectations
- Competitor data files (YAML) for reuse.
- Full page drafts with meta tags, tables, and CTAs.
- A prioritized plan of pages to create.

## Pitfalls to avoid
- Biased or misleading comparisons.
- Feature checklists without real explanation.
- No update process for pricing and features.

## When to pair with other skills
- programmatic-seo: scale comparisons across many competitors.
- seo-audit: optimize comparison pages post-launch.
- schema-markup: FAQ or comparison schema.
